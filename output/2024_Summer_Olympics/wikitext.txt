{{2024 Summer Olympics}}
The '''2024 Summer Olympics''' ({{langx|fr|Les Jeux Olympiques d'été de 2024}}), officially the '''Games of the XXXIII Olympiad''' ({{langx|fr|Jeux de la XXXIIIe olympiade de l'ère moderne}}) and branded as '''Paris 2024''', were an international [[multi-sport event]] held from 26&nbsp;July to 11&nbsp;August 2024 in [[France]], with several events started from 24 July. [[Paris]] was the host city, with events (mainly [[Football at the 2024 Summer Olympics|football]]) held in 16 additional cities spread across [[metropolitan France]], including the [[Sailing at the 2024 Summer Olympics|sailing]] centre in the second-largest city of France, [[Marseille]], on the [[Mediterranean Sea]], as well as one subsite for [[Surfing at the 2024 Summer Olympics|surfing]] in [[Tahiti]], French Polynesia.<ref>{{cite web|last=Butler|first=Nick|date=7 February 2018|title=Paris 2024 to start week earlier than planned after IOC approve date change|url=https://www.insidethegames.biz/articles/1061155/paris-2024-to-start-week-earlier-than-planned-after-ioc-approve-date-change|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201109023013/https://www.insidethegames.biz/articles/1061155/paris-2024-to-start-week-earlier-than-planned-after-ioc-approve-date-change|archive-date=9 November 2020|access-date=7 February 2018|website=Inside the Games}}</ref>

Paris was awarded the Games at the [[131st IOC Session]] in [[Lima]], Peru, on 13&nbsp;September 2017. After multiple withdrawals that left only [[Paris bid for the 2024 Summer Olympics|Paris]] and [[Los Angeles bid for the 2024 Summer Olympics|Los Angeles]] in contention, the [[International Olympic Committee]] (IOC) approved a process to concurrently award the 2024 and [[2028 Summer Olympics]] to the two remaining candidate cities; both bids were praised for their high technical plans and innovative ways to use a record-breaking number of existing and temporary facilities. Having previously hosted in [[1900 Summer Olympics|1900]] and [[1924 Summer Olympics|1924]], Paris became the second city ever to host the [[Summer Olympics]] three times (after [[London]], which hosted the games in [[1908 Summer Olympics|1908]], [[1948 Summer Olympics|1948]], and [[2012 Summer Olympics|2012]]).<ref>{{Cite news|date=21 July 2024|title=When do the Summer Olympics start? What you need to know ahead of Paris 2024|url=https://www.abc.net.au/news/2024-07-22/what-you-need-to-know-ahead-of-paris-2024-summer-olympics/103963744|access-date=23 July 2024|work=ABC News AU|language=en-AU|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240721220513/https://www.abc.net.au/news/2024-07-22/what-you-need-to-know-ahead-of-paris-2024-summer-olympics/103963744|archive-date=21 July 2024|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|date=22 July 2024|title=Paris 2024: How is France preparing for the Olympics and Paralympics?|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-68018536|access-date=23 July 2024|work=BBC News|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240722190530/https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-68018536|archive-date=22 July 2024|url-status=live}}</ref> Paris 2024 marked the [[centenary]] of the 1924 games and the [[1924 Winter Olympics]] in [[Chamonix]] (the first [[Winter Olympic Games|Winter Olympics]]), as well as the sixth Olympic Games hosted by France (three Summer Olympics and three Winter Olympics) and the first with this distinction since the [[1992 Winter Olympics|1992 Winter Games]] in [[Albertville]]. The Summer Games returned to the traditional four-year [[Olympiad]] cycle, after the [[2020 Summer Olympics|2020 edition]] was postponed to 2021 due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].

The [[United States at the 2024 Summer Olympics|United States]] topped the [[2024 Summer Olympics medal table|medal table]] for the fourth consecutive Summer Games and [[List of Olympic teams by medals won#List by team|19th time overall]], with 40 gold and 126 total medals.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-08-11 |title=USA top medal table after winning thrilling last gold |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/olympics/articles/c80evjzy2jlo |website=BBC |language=en |access-date=13 August 2024 |archive-date=12 August 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240812022213/https://www.bbc.com/sport/olympics/articles/c80evjzy2jlo |url-status=live }}</ref> [[China at the 2024 Summer Olympics|China]] tied with the United States on gold (40), but finished second due to having fewer silvers; the nation won 91 medals overall. This is the first time a gold medal tie among the two most successful nations has occurred in Summer Olympic history.<ref>{{Cite web |first=Gabe |last=Whisnant |date=2024-08-11 |title=Olympic medal count shows China made history after battle with Team USA |url=https://www.newsweek.com/olympic-medal-count-show-china-making-history-team-usa-cant-stop-them-1937541 |access-date=2024-09-09 |website=Newsweek |language=en |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240811142751/https://www.newsweek.com/olympic-medal-count-show-china-making-history-team-usa-cant-stop-them-1937541 |archive-date=11 August 2024 |url-status=live}}</ref> [[Japan at the 2024 Summer Olympics|Japan]] finished third with 20 gold medals and sixth in the overall medal count. [[Australia at the 2024 Summer Olympics|Australia]] finished fourth with 18 gold medals and fifth in the overall medal count. The host nation, [[France at the 2024 Summer Olympics|France]], finished fifth with 16 gold and 64 total medals, and fourth in the overall medal count. [[Dominica at the 2024 Summer Olympics|Dominica]], [[Saint Lucia at the 2024 Summer Olympics|Saint Lucia]], [[Cape Verde at the 2024 Summer Olympics|Cape Verde]] and [[Albania at the 2024 Summer Olympics|Albania]] won their first-ever Olympic medals, the former two both being gold, with [[Botswana at the 2024 Summer Olympics|Botswana]] and [[Guatemala at the 2024 Summer Olympics|Guatemala]] also winning their first-ever gold medals. The [[Refugee Olympic Team at the 2024 Summer Olympics|Refugee Olympic Team]] also won their first-ever medal, a bronze in boxing.

==Host selection==
{{further|Bids for the 2024 and 2028 Summer Olympics}}
[[File:Olympic rings on the Eiffel Tower 2024 (19).jpg|thumb|The Olympic rings on the [[Eiffel Tower]] during the Games]]

Having previously hosted the 1900 and 1924 Games, Paris did not attempt to host the Olympics again until it bid, unsuccessfully, for the [[1992 Summer Olympics|1992 Games]] which were awarded to [[Barcelona]]. Subsequent bids for the [[2008 Summer Olympics|2008]] and [[2012 Summer Olympics|2012]] Games were also unsuccessful, as they were awarded to [[Beijing]] and [[London]], respectively. Undeterred, Paris decided to bid once more for the 2024 edition, which would mark the centenary of its last Games.

The six candidate cities were [[Paris bid for the 2024 Summer Olympics|Paris]], [[Hamburg bid for the 2024 Summer Olympics|Hamburg]], [[Boston bid for the 2024 Summer Olympics|Boston]], [[Budapest bid for the 2024 Summer Olympics|Budapest]], [[Rome bid for the 2024 Summer Olympics|Rome]], and [[Los Angeles bid for the 2024 and 2028 Summer Olympics|Los Angeles]]. The bidding process was slowed by withdrawals, political uncertainty, and rising costs. Boston surpassed Los Angeles, San Francisco, and Washington, DC, for the official U.S. bid. On 27&nbsp;July 2015, Boston and the [[United States Olympic & Paralympic Committee|USOC]] mutually agreed to terminate Boston's bid to host the Games, partly because of mixed feelings among city residents. ===Host city election===
Paris was elected as the host city on 13 September 2017 at the [[131st IOC Session]] in [[Lima]], Peru. The two French IOC members, [[Guy Drut]] and [[Tony Estanguet]], were ineligible to vote under the rules of the [[Olympic Charter]].<ref>{{cite web|website=IOC|date=13 September 2017|access-date=23 May 2024|title=IOC Session in Lima opened with energetic ceremony|url=https://olympics.com/ioc/news/ioc-session-in-lima-opened-with-energetic-ceremony|archive-date=13 May 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210513211228/https://olympics.com/ioc/news/ioc-session-in-lima-opened-with-energetic-ceremony|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=IOC makes historic decision by simultaneously awarding Olympic Games 2024 to Paris and 2028 to Los Angeles|url=https://olympics.com/ioc/news/ioc-makes-historic-decision-by-simultaneously-awarding-olympic-games-2024-to-paris-and-2028-to-los-angeles|date=13 September 2017|access-date=23 May 2024|archive-date=12 May 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210512082624/https://olympics.com/ioc/news/ioc-makes-historic-decision-by-simultaneously-awarding-olympic-games-2024-to-paris-and-2028-to-los-angelesy|url-status=live}}</ref>

Most of the Olympic events were held in the city of Paris and [[Île-de-France|its metropolitan region]], including the neighbouring cities of [[Saint-Denis, Seine-Saint-Denis|Saint-Denis]], [[Le Bourget]], [[Nanterre]], [[Versailles, Yvelines|Versailles]], and [[Vaires-sur-Marne]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Paris 2024 Competition Venue Concept Map|url=https://www.paris2024.org/en/competition-venue-concept/|access-date=18 March 2024|website=Paris 2024|language=en|archive-date=18 March 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240318015927/https://www.paris2024.org/en/competition-venue-concept/|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Paris 2024 Les Sites des Compètition|url=https://www.paris2024.org/fr/sites-de-competition/|access-date=18 March 2024|website=Paris 2024|language=fr|archive-date=18 March 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240318020001/https://www.paris2024.org/fr/sites-de-competition/|url-status=live}}</ref>

The basketball preliminaries and handball finals were held in [[Lille]], {{cvt|225|km|mi}} from the host city, Paris; the sailing and some of the football games were held in the Mediterranean city of [[Marseille]], which is {{cvt|777|km|mi}} from Paris; meanwhile, the surfing events were held in [[Teahupo'o]] village in the overseas territory of [[Tahiti]], [[French Polynesia]], which is {{cvt|15716|km|mi}} from Paris. Football was also hosted in an additional five cities: [[Bordeaux]], [[Décines-Charpieu]] ([[Metropolis of Lyon|Lyon]]), [[Nantes]], [[Nice]] and [[Saint-Étienne]], some of which are home to {{nowrap|[[Ligue 1]]}} clubs.

===Medals===
[[File:Medailles-olympiques-Paris2024.jpg|thumb|Medals from the Games, with a piece of the [[Eiffel Tower]]]]
The president of the Paris 2024 Olympic Organizing Committee, Tony Estanguet, unveiled the [[Olympic medal|Olympic and Paralympic medals]] for the Games in February 2024, which on the obverse featured embedded hexagon-shaped tokens of scrap iron that had been taken from the original construction of the [[Eiffel Tower]], with the logo of the Games engraved into it.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/olympics/68239167|work=BBC Sport|title=Paris 2024: Eiffel Tower metal in Olympics and Paralympics medals|date=8 February 2024|access-date=8 February 2024|archive-date=8 February 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240208115449/https://www.bbc.com/sport/olympics/68239167|url-status=live}}</ref> Approximately 5,084 medals would be produced by the French [[Mint (facility)|mint]] [[Monnaie de Paris]], and were designed by [[Chaumet]], a luxury jewellery firm based in Paris.<ref>{{cite web|last=Theissen|first=Marion|date=18 July 2024|title=Paris 2024: the Olympic and Paralympic medals have been revealed|url=https://www.sportstiger.com/paris-2024-olympics/medal-tally|access-date=18 July 2024|website=SportsTiger|archive-date=18 July 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240718104050/https://www.sportstiger.com/paris-2024-olympics/medal-tally|url-status=live}}</ref>

The reverse of the medals features [[Nike (mythology)|Nike]], the Greek goddess of victory, inside the [[Panathenaic Stadium]] which hosted the [[1896 Summer Olympics|first modern Olympics]] in 1896. Parthenon and the Eiffel Tower can also be seen in the background on both sides of the medal.<ref>{{cite news|date=8 February 2024|title=Paris Olympic and Paralympic medals will contain chunks of Eiffel Tower|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2024/feb/08/paris-olympic-and-paralympic-medals-will-contain-chunks-of-eiffel-tower|access-date=8 February 2024|work=The Guardian|agency=Reuters|archive-date=8 February 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240208110259/https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2024/feb/08/paris-olympic-and-paralympic-medals-will-contain-chunks-of-eiffel-tower|url-status=live}}</ref> Each medal weighs {{cvt|455|–|529|g|oz|0}}, has a diameter of {{cvt|85|mm|in}} and is {{cvt|9.2|mm|in}} thick.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://news.sky.com/story/paris-2024-first-look-at-olympic-and-paralympic-medals-featuring-chunks-of-eiffel-tower-13066755|work=Sky News|title=Paris 2024: First look at Olympic and Paralympic medals featuring chunks of Eiffel Tower|date=8 February 2024|access-date=8 February 2024|archive-date=8 February 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240208133234/https://news.sky.com/story/paris-2024-first-look-at-olympic-and-paralympic-medals-featuring-chunks-of-eiffel-tower-13066755|url-status=live}}</ref> The gold medals are made with 98.8 percent silver and 1.13 percent gold, while the bronze medals are made up with copper, zinc, and tin.<ref>{{cite news|date=8 February 2024|title=Paris 2024 unveils Paralympic and Olympic Games medals|url=https://www.paralympic.org/news/paris-2024-unveils-paralympic-and-olympic-games-medals|access-date=8 February 2024|work=Paralympics|archive-date=8 February 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240208110601/https://www.paralympic.org/news/paris-2024-unveils-paralympic-and-olympic-games-medals|url-status=live}}</ref>

===Security===
France reached an agreement with [[Europol]] and the [[UK Home Office]] to help strengthen security and "facilitate operational information exchange and international law enforcement cooperation" during the Games.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.europol.europa.eu/media-press/newsroom/news/olympics-2024-security-topped|work=Europol|title=Olympics 2024 security topped up|date=31 October 2023|access-date=8 February 2024|archive-date=4 December 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231204043109/https://www.europol.europa.eu/media-press/newsroom/news/olympics-2024-security-topped|url-status=live}}</ref> The agreement included a plan to deploy more drones and sea barriers to prevent small boats from crossing [[the Channel]] illegally.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2024/jan/30/british-police-and-security-services-to-help-protect-paris-olympics|work=The Guardian|title=British police and security services to help protect Paris Olympics|date=30 January 2024|access-date=9 February 2024|author=Syal, Rajeev|archive-date=8 February 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240208202836/https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2024/jan/30/british-police-and-security-services-to-help-protect-paris-olympics|url-status=live}}</ref> The [[British Army]] would also provide support by deploying [[Starstreak]] surface-to-air missile units for air security.<ref>{{cite news|title=Starstreak manufacturer Thales sees weapons production double due to Ukraine|url=https://www.forces.net/technology/weapons-and-kit/nlaw-and-starstreak-manufacturer-thales-sees-weapons-production-double|access-date=3 April 2024|work=[[Forces News]]|date=25 March 2024|archive-date=3 April 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240403173331/https://www.forces.net/technology/weapons-and-kit/nlaw-and-starstreak-manufacturer-thales-sees-weapons-production-double|url-status=live}}</ref> To prepare for the Games, the Paris police held inspections and rehearsals in their bomb disposal unit, similar to their preparations for the [[2023 Rugby World Cup]] at the [[Stade de France]].<ref name="F24">{{cite news|url=https://www.france24.com/en/france/20231205-paris-police-expect-2024-olympics-to-be-considerable-challenge-for-bomb-disposal-squad|work=France 24|title=Paris police expect 2024 Olympics to be 'considerable challenge' for bomb disposal squad|date=5 December 2023|access-date=9 February 2024|archive-date=31 January === Food ===
To reduce the environmental impact and climate footprint of the Games,<ref name="Food Vision" /> the Olympic venues served twice as much [[Plant-based diet|plant-based]] food as was available in [[2012 Summer Olympics|London in 2012]] and [[2016 Summer Olympics|Rio in 2016]].<ref>{{Cite web|last1=Varley|first1=Ciaran|last2=Lockwood|first2=Dave|date=23 July 2024|title=Recycled bottle tops and tables made from shuttlecocks – the greenest Olympics?|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/articles/cm52drr0lp1o|access-date=23 July 2024|website=BBC Sport|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240723183323/https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/articles/cm52drr0lp1o|archive-date=23 July 2024|url-status=live}}</ref> [[Chicken nugget#Vegan chicken nuggets|Vegan chicken nuggets]] and [[vegan hot dog]]s were served in place of the meat-based variety.<ref name="chicken nuggets">{{Cite news|last=Ingle|first=Sean|date=22 July 2024|title=Faster, higher, stronger ... and healthier: chicken nuggets off menu at Paris 2024|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/article/2024/jul/22/chicken-nuggets-paris-2024-olympic-games-food-health|access-date=23 July 2024|work=The Guardian|issn=0261-3077|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240722214012/https://www.theguardian.com/sport/article/2024/jul/22/chicken-nuggets-paris-2024-olympic-games-food-health|archive-date=22 July 2024|url-status=live}}</ref> Venues for spectators served on average two-thirds plant-based meals. The [[Place de la Concorde]], the venue that hosted the skateboarding, breakdancing and BMX events, only served plant-based food. The football stadiums served 40% plant-based food. About 30% of the meals served to athletes in the Olympic Village were plant-based.<ref>{{Cite web|title=The race to plant-based food: How the Paris Olympics became the most vegan ever|url=https://www.bbc.com/future/article/20240808-vive-la-vegan-the-paris-olympics-race-to-meat-free|access-date=2024-08-12|website=www.bbc.com|date=9 August 2024 |language=en-GB|archive-date=12 August 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240812063842/https://www.bbc.com/future/article/20240808-vive-la-vegan-the-paris-olympics-race-to-meat-free|url-status=live }}</ref>

A prior estimate of 13 million meals were served at the Games;<ref name="Food Vision">{{Cite web|title=Paris 2024 Food Vision|url=https://olympics.com/en/paris-2024/our-commitments/the-environment/food-vision|website=Paris 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240423064502/https://olympics.com/en/paris-2024/our-commitments/the-environment/food-vision|archive-date=23 April 2024|url-status=live}}</ref> with around 40,000 meals each day, 1,200 of those will be [[Michelin-starred]].<ref name="chicken nuggets" /> Each day, a [[boulangerie]] will bake fresh [[baguette]]s and other [[bread]]s.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Corbet|first=Sylvie|date=1 May 2024|orig-date=30 April 2024|title=Paris Olympic athletes will feast on freshly baked bread, select cheeses and plenty of veggies|url=https://apnews.com/article/olympics-paris-french-food-vegetarian-f863445f5b44d868b6949f34f5f70403|access-date=23 July 2024|website=AP News|language=en-US|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240514203952/https://apnews.com/article/olympics-paris-french-food-vegetarian-f863445f5b44d868b6949f34f5f70403|archive-date=14 May 2024|url-status=live}}</ref> A 3,500-seat restaurant was constructed for the Games to highlight global cuisine. Great Britain's team asked for porridge to be added to the menu, and South Korea's team asked for [[kimchi]].<ref name="chicken nuggets" />

In the 2024 Paris Olympics, several new events and formats have been introduced. [[Formula Kite]] made its debut, described as the "Formula One of the Olympics", featuring high-speed foil racing with separate events for men and women.<ref>{{cite news|last=Smith|first=Alexander|date=4 August 2024|title=Olympics-Sailing-Kiteboarding comes of age as the Formula One of the Games|url=https://www.reuters.com/sports/olympics/olympics-sailing-kiteboarding-comes-age-formula-one-games-2024-08-04/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240807120000/https://www.reuters.com/sports/olympics/olympics-sailing-kiteboarding-comes-age-formula-one-games-2024-08-04/|archive-date=7 August 2024|access-date=8 August 2024|work=Reuters}}</ref> Kayak cross also debuted, where four athletes race against each other on a course with multiple gates, marking the first head-to-head race in Olympic canoe slalom history. Changes in other sports included the introduction of men's participation in artistic swimming, a new women's weight class in boxing, and the addition of a marathon race walk mixed relay in track and field.<ref>{{cite web|date=7 August 2023|title=New Sports and Events in the 2024 Olympics|url=https://www.nbcphiladelphia.com/paris-2024-summer-olympics/new-sports-events-2024-olympics-paris/3906086/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240807120000/https://www.nbcphiladelphia.com/paris-2024-summer-olympics/new-sports-events-2024-olympics-paris/3906086/|archive-date=7 August 2024|access-date=8 August 2024|work=NBC Philadelphia}}</ref>

=== Medal reallocations from previous Olympics ===

Due to new IOC rules and protocols, one specific medal awarding ceremony was held in this venue [[Figure skating at the 2022 Winter Olympics – Team event|figure skating team event]] from the [[2022 Winter Olympics]] in Beijing. It had been the first Olympic medal ceremony to be delayed after [[Kamila Valieva]] from original gold medalist Russia was reported and then confirmed to have tested positive in 2021 for [[trimetazidine]]. ==Participating National Olympic Committees==
204 out of 206 [[National Olympic Committees]] are represented at the 2024 Summer Games with 54 from [[Association of National Olympic Committees of Africa|Africa]], 48 from [[European Olympic Committees|Europe]], 44 from [[Olympic Council of Asia|Asia]], 41 from the [[Panam Sports|Americas]] and 17 from [[Oceania National Olympic Committees|Oceania]]. ==Marketing==

===Emblem===
The emblem for the 2024 Summer Olympics and Paralympics was unveiled on 21&nbsp;October 2019 at the [[Grand Rex]]. Inspired by [[Art Deco in Paris|Art Deco]],<ref name="auto1"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.latimes.com/sports/story/2019-10-24/paris-olympics-2024-logo|title=Is it an Olympic logo? Or a coquettish scamp?|date=24 October 2019|website=Los Angeles Times|language=en-US|access-date=25 October 2019|archive-date=18 February 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230218131636/https://www.latimes.com/sports/story/2019-10-24/paris-olympics-2024-logo|url-status=live|author=Wharton, David}}</ref> it is a representation of [[Marianne]], the [[national personification]] of France, with a flame formed in [[negative space]] by her hair. The emblem also resembles a gold medal. Tony Estanguet explained that the emblem symbolised "the power and the magic of the Games", and the Games being "for people". The use of a female figure also serves as an homage to the [[1900 Summer Olympics]] in Paris, which were the first to [[Participation of women in the Olympics|allow women to participate]].<ref name="BBC20191022">{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-50135652|title=Olympic flame or dating ad? Paris 2024 logo divides opinion|date=22 October 2019|work=BBC News|access-date=25 October 2019|archive-date=14 May 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230514070728/https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-50135652|url-status=live}}</ref> The emblem was designed by the French designer Sylvain Boyer<ref name="auto1">{{Cite web|last=Williams|first=Eliza|date=3 December 2019|title=Trends of 2019: Branding and logos|url=https://www.creativereview.co.uk/trends-of-2019-branding-and-logos/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201023111125/https://www.creativereview.co.uk/trends-of-2019-branding-and-logos/|archive-date=23 October 2020|access-date=7 December 2020|website=Creative Review}}</ref> with the French design agencies [[Royalties (brand management agency)|Royalties & Ecobranding]].<ref>{{cite web|date=21 October 2019|title=How Paris 2024 unveiled its new logo?|url=https://www.en24.news/2019/10/how-paris-2024-unveiled-its-new-logo.html|access-date=22 October 2019|website=en24.news|archive-date=22 October 2019|url-status=usurped|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191022144147/https://www.en24.news/2019/10/how-paris-2024-unveiled-its-new-logo.html}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Lepeltier|first=Nicolas|date=21 October 2019|title=Paris 2024: le logo des Jeux Olympique's dévoilé|trans-title=Paris 2024: Olympic Games logo unveiled|url=https://www.lemonde.fr/sport/article/2019/10/21/paris-2024-le-nouveau-logo-des-jeux-olympiques-se-devoile_6016373_3242.html|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230218131633/https://www.lemonde.fr/sport/article/2019/10/21/paris-2024-le-nouveau-logo-des-jeux-olympiques-se-devoile_6016373_3242.html|archive-date=18 February 2023|access-date=22 October 2019|newspaper=Le Monde|language=fr}}</ref><ref name="auto1" />

The emblem for Paris 2024 was considered the biggest new logo release of 2019 by many design magazines.<ref name="auto1"/><ref>{{cite web|last=Wong|first=Henry|date=6 December 2019|title=The 10 biggest rebrands and logo designs of 2019|url=https://www.designweek.co.uk/issues/2-8-december-2019/the-10-biggest-rebrands-and-logo-designs-of-2019/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230218131659/https://www.designweek.co.uk/issues/2-8-december-2019/the-10-biggest-rebrands-and-logo-designs-of-2019/|archive-date=18 February 2023|access-date=7 December 2020|website=Design Week|language=en-US}}</ref> An Opinion Way survey showed that 83 per cent of French people say they liked the new Paris 2024 Games emblem. Approval ratings were high, with 82 per cent of those surveyed finding it aesthetically appealing and 78 per cent finding it to be creative.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.paris2024.org/en/content/83-of-french-people-approve-of-new-paris-2024-games-emblem/|title=83% of French People Approve of New Paris 2024 Games Emblem|date=24 October 2019|website=Paris 2024|language=en-US|access-date=19 August 2021|archive-date=19 September 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200919024929/https://www.paris2024.org/en/content/83-of-french-people-approve-of-new-paris-2024-games-emblem/}}</ref> It was met with some mockery on social media, one user commenting that the logo "would be better suited to a dating site or a hair salon".<ref name="BBC20191022" />

For the first time, the corresponding Paralympics shared the same emblem as the Olympics, with no difference bar the governing bodies' logo, reflecting a shared "ambition" between both events.<ref>{{cite news|last=Rowbottom|first=Mike|date=21 October 2019|title=Paris 2024 unveil new shared Olympic and Paralympic Games emblem|url=http://www.insidethegames.biz/articles/1086210/paris-2024-new-emblem-launch|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191022034112/https://www.insidethegames.biz/articles/1086210/paris-2024-new-emblem-launch|archive-date=22 October 2019|access-date=22 October 2019|website=Inside the Games}}</ref>

===Posters===
[[File:AfficheJO Parc-Urbain.jpg|thumb|Official poster of the Paris 2024 Olympic and Paralympic Games on display at the Parc urbain de la Concorde]]
The [[Olympic poster]] for these games was revealed on 4&nbsp;March 2024. Designed by Ugo Gattoni, the poster uses a diptych design, with one half representing the Olympics and the other half representing the Paralympics. For the first time in Summer Games history, the Olympic poster and Paralympic poster were designed together, as each one can work independently as halves, or be combined into one poster all together. The posters took 2,000 hours, across six months to complete.<ref>{{cite web|title=Paris 2024 Iconic Posters|url=https://www.paris2024.org/en/iconic-posters/|access-date=6 March 2024|website=Paris 2024|language=en|archive-date=6 March 2024|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240306080855/https://www.paris2024.org/en/iconic-posters/|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Muñana|first=Gustavo|date=5 March 2024|title='Surrealist' diptych poster for Paris 2024 Olympics|url=https://www.insidethegames.biz/articles/1144080/surrealist-diptych-poster-for-paris|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240305111056/https://www.insidethegames.biz/articles/1144080/surrealist-diptych-poster-for-paris|archive-date=5 March 2024|access-date=7 March 2024|website=Inside the Games|url-status=live}}</ref>

===Opening ceremony===
A portion of the [[2024 Summer Olympics opening ceremony|opening ceremony of the Paris 2024 Olympics]] sparked significant controversy worldwide for its inclusion of a reenactment of the [[Feast of the Gods (art)|feast of the Gods]], which depicts various Greek gods partaking in a banquet at [[Mount Olympus]]. The performance faced heavy backlash from [[Christian]] and conservative groups, who accused the performance representing the feast of Dionysus as mocking Leonardo da Vinci's ''[[The Last Supper (Leonardo)|The Last Supper]]''.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Zhuang |first=Yan |date=2024-07-28 |title=An Olympics Scene Draws Scorn. Did It Really Parody 'The Last Supper'? |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2024/07/28/sports/olympics-opening-ceremony-last-supper-paris.html |access-date=2025-02-17 |work=The New York Times |language=en-US |issn=0362-4331}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-08-12 |title=Olympics opening ceremony tableau sparks controversy among Christian community |url=https://www.today.com/today/rcna163929 |access-date=2025-02-17 |website=TODAY.com |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-07-29 |title=Paris Olympics organizers say sorry for offense, but insist opening ceremony did not depict "The Last Supper" - CBS News |url=https://www.cbsnews.com/news/paris-olympics-organizers-apologize-last-supper-tableau-religious-conservatives/ |access-date=2025-02-17 |website=www.cbsnews.com |language=en-US}}</ref>

The artistic director of the ceremony, [[Thomas Jolly]], the ceremony's creative team, and many art historians later confirmed that the performance was unrelated to Leonardo da Vinci's The Last Supper. The Olympic World Library would later publish a media guide (written before the ceremony) describing it as being a homage to cultural festivities.<ref>{{cite web |title=OPENING CEREMONY OF THE PARIS 2024 OLYMPIC GAMES : MEDIA GUIDE : 26-07-24, 19:30 / PARIS 2024 ORGANISING COMMITTEE FOR THE OLYMPIC AND PARALYMPIC GAMES |url=https://library.olympics.com/Default/doc/SYRACUSE/3415954/opening-ceremony-of-the-paris-2024-olympic-games-media-guide-26-07-24-19-30-paris-2024-organising-co |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240903075353/https://library.olympics.com/Default/doc/SYRACUSE/3415954/opening-ceremony-of-the-paris-2024-olympic-games-media-guide-26-07-24-19-30-paris-2024-organising-co |archive-date=3 September 2024 |publisher=Olympic World Library}}</ref> According to Georgian fact checking website Myth Detector, many experts had pointed out the differences between ''The Last Supper'' fresco and the performance.<ref>{{cite web |title=THE LAST SUPPER OR GREEK MYTHOLOGY – WHAT DOES THE SCENE AT THE PARIS OLYMPICS DEPICT AND WHY DID THE ORGANIZERS APOLOGIZE? |url=https://mythdetector.ge/en/the-last-supper-or-greek-mythology/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240808123741/https://mythdetector.ge/en/the-last-supper-or-greek-mythology/ |archive-date=8 August 2024 |access-date=29 July 2024 |publisher=Myth Detector}}</ref>
